## Regulatory Environment: Most Favorable in 15 Years

Three landmark policy shifts have opened the window:

1. **EO 130 (April 2021)** — Lifted the nine-year moratorium on new mineral agreements
2. **Open-pit mining ban lifted (December 2021)** — Removed the blanket prohibition on open-pit operations
3. **RA 12253 (September 2025)** — "Enhanced Fiscal Regime for Large-Scale Metallic Mining Act" — first unified, predictable tax framework in Philippine mining history

## Permit Architecture

### Three Types of Mineral Agreements

| Agreement Type | Filipino Ownership Req. | Term | Revenue Split | Best For |
|---------------|------------------------|------|---------------|----------|
| **MPSA** (Mineral Production Sharing Agreement) | ≥60% Filipino | 25 + 25 years | Government receives share of production | Filipino-majority JVs |
| **FTAA** (Financial or Technical Assistance Agreement) | No minimum — 100% foreign allowed | 25 + 25 years | **60% government / 40% contractor** (net revenue after cost recovery) | Foreign-led large-scale ($50M+ capex) |
| **JV/Co-Production** | Varies | 25 + 25 years | Negotiated per agreement | State-partnered ventures |

**MPSA is the preferred structure** for a Filipino-majority partnership. It avoids the punitive 60-40 FTAA revenue split. Only 5 FTAAs have ever been issued in the Philippines.

### The Mineral Processing Permit (MPP) — Strategic Entry Point

**Critical finding:** Mineral processing can be permitted separately from mining extraction. An MPP allows a company to process ore sourced from MPSA/FTAA holders under a supply agreement, **without holding its own mining rights.**

This is the strategic entry point for Aboitiz:
- A separate Philippine-incorporated entity obtains the MPP from MGB
- It sources ore from existing MPSA/FTAA holders under supply agreements
- It builds and operates the processing plant (construction by ACI; maintenance by ACI Plus)
- It does NOT hold mining rights or undertake extraction — avoiding the political, environmental, and reputational risks of mining itself
- AboitizPower supplies energy; AIC provides infrastructure; ADI optimizes processing
- 60% Filipino ownership easily met by an Aboitiz-led JV

**Precedent:** This is exactly the model CD Processing Ltd. used at Carmen Copper in Toledo City — a British company that obtained BOI pioneer project approval for a ₱583M copper ore sorting plant, sourcing run-of-mine ore from the existing mining operation, with Aboitiz Construction building the facility.

**MPP application requires:**
1. Application form (MGB Form No. 11-02) with filing fee
2. Feasibility study with plant design, technology, anti-pollution measures
3. ECC or Certificate of Exemption
4. NCIP Certification Precondition (if in indigenous peoples' area)
5. LGU endorsement
6. Proof of financial capability (audited financials, credit lines)
7. Supply contract with ore source (MPSA holder)
8. Five-year activity history of applicant

### Required Permits — Full Sequence (for extraction + processing)

| Step | Permit/Requirement | Issuing Agency | Timeline |
|------|--------------------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | **FPIC** (Free and Prior Informed Consent) | NCIP | 6–18 months |
| 2 | **Exploration Permit (EP)** | MGB | 2 years, renewable |
| 3 | **Declaration of Mining Project Feasibility (DMPF)** | Filed with MGB | Part of EP term |
| 4 | **Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC)** | EMB under DENR | 6–12 months |
| 5 | **MPSA or FTAA approval** | DENR Secretary / President (FTAA) | Variable |
| 6 | **LGU Endorsements** | Provincial, Municipal, Barangay | Required before permit issuance |
| 7 | **Mining Permit / Mineral Agreement** | DENR-MGB | Runs concurrently |
| 8 | **Mineral Processing Permit (MPP)** | MGB Regional Office | Separate process if processing-only |
| 9 | **Environmental Work Programme (EnWP)** | DENR-EMB | Pre-implementation |
| 10 | **Safety and Health Programme (SHP)** | MGB | Pre-implementation |
| 11 | **Community Development Programme (CDP)** | MGB | Pre-implementation, annually updated |

**FPIC is the longest lead time and highest risk permit.** Without genuine indigenous community consent, no project proceeds.

### Timeline Reality

DENR is targeting a 2-year permit timeline with digital reforms rolled out in CARAGA and Davao. The actual track record:

- **Celsius Resources MCB Project**: MPSA approved March 2024 — described as "the first copper project approved in the Philippines in fifteen years"
- **Silangan (Philex)**: Over a decade from permitting to first production (Q1 2026)
- **Tampakan**: Identified 30+ years ago, still not in production

**Realistic planning: 3–5 years from application to approved mineral agreement** under the current regime. Parallel processing (MPSA application proceeds while NCIP and LGU endorsements are pending) is now permitted, which should compress timelines.

## New Fiscal Regime (RA 12253)

Signed by President Marcos on September 4, 2025.

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Royalty (inside mineral reservations)** | 5% of gross output |
| **Royalty (outside mineral reservations)** | Margin-based royalty on income |
| **Windfall tax** | Applied when profit margins exceed 30% |
| **Excise tax** | 4% on minerals (TRAIN Act 2017, retained) |
| **Separate taxable entity** | Each mining project treated individually — no cross-project loss offsetting |
| **LGU share** | 40% of gross collections from excise taxes, royalties, and related charges |
| **MGB/research allocation** | 10% of royalties for exploration, research, environmental protection |
| **Audit authority** | BIR and BOC authorized to audit past mineral sales and exports |

Chamber of Mines endorsed the law as establishing a "stable, transparent, and competitive fiscal environment." Marcos framed it as ensuring "the wealth from our soil should transform into schools for our children, hospitals for our families, and roads that connect our people with opportunities."

## Partnership Structure Models

| Structure | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **MPSA with Filipino-majority JV** | Foreign investor ≤40% in Filipino-incorporated JV holding MPSA | Most common for mid-size projects |
| **FTAA (100% foreign)** | Government grants directly to foreign corp; 60-40 revenue split | OceanaGold (Didipio) — first FTAA revenue payment ₱1.129B in 2024 |
| **Service/Build-Operate** | Construction firm builds and maintains processing plant under contract | THPAL model: ACI built and maintains the SMM/NAC plant |
| **MPP-based processing** | Separate entity processes ore from MPSA holders under supply agreement | CD Processing Ltd. at Carmen Copper (₱583M) |
| **Technology partnership** | Foreign partner provides tech; Filipino partner provides land, permits, community | Tampakan seeking "advanced technology" partner |

## Risk Assessment: Southern Mindanao

### Indigenous Peoples & Community Opposition — SINGLE HIGHEST RISK FACTOR

- Nearly two-thirds of all indigenous land claimed by mining companies for critical minerals is on Mindanao (Global Witness)
- CARAGA described as "one of the most dangerous regions in the Philippines to be an Indigenous anti-mining activist"
- Lumad peoples (Manobo, B'laan, T'boli, Subanen, Mandaya) have ancestral domain claims across vast areas
- FPIC is legally required but the process is contested — communities argue it is often coerced
- Tampakan faces strong Lumad resistance even after decades of engagement

**Mitigation model:** THPAL — 1,000 local jobs, ₱13M+ annual SDMP funding, ACI as top taxpayer in Claver for 3 consecutive years. Key: genuine, sustained community investment that predates operations — not post-hoc CSR.

### Political & Regulatory Risk

| Risk | Assessment |
|------|------------|
| National policy reversal | **Low** — bipartisan support; RA 12253 passed with broad backing |
| LGU opposition | **Medium** — local politicians can delay/block endorsements |
| DENR permit delays | **Medium** — digital reforms underway but capacity varies by region |
| **2028 presidential election** | **Medium** — policy continuity uncertain after Marcos |
| Fiscal regime change | **Low near term** — RA 12253 just signed Sep 2025 |

### Environmental Risk

| Risk | Assessment |
|------|------------|
| Typhoon exposure | **High** — Mindanao eastern seaboard faces Pacific typhoons annually |
| Tailings management | **High** — TSF failures are the catastrophic risk; requires world-class engineering |
| Water contamination | **High** — acid mine drainage and processing chemicals require rigorous management |
| Reforestation obligations | **Medium** — DENR requires mine rehabilitation and progressive reclamation |
| Climate scrutiny | **Rising** — Philippines lost forest cover "three times the size of New York City" in mining zones |

### Security Risk

| Risk | Assessment |
|------|------------|
| NPA/communist insurgency | **Declining** but not eliminated in CARAGA |
| Clan/political violence | **Localized** in some mining areas |
| Illegal small-scale mining | **Present** — areas with known mineralization attract informal miners |

## Strategic Synthesis

For a 6,000-acre Southern Mindanao project:

1. **Use MPSA structure** (≥60% Filipino-owned JV) — avoids heavier FTAA revenue split
2. **Secure FPIC early** — longest lead time, most politically sensitive; without genuine consent, nothing proceeds
3. **Leverage the MPP pathway** for mineral processing as lower-risk entry avoiding extraction-stage controversies
4. **Budget 3–5 years** from application to approved mineral agreement
5. **Model for new fiscal regime**: 5% royalty on gross output, windfall tax above 30% margins
6. **Pair with ACI** — Mindanao presence, safety track record, community credibility
7. **Address community risk structurally**: SDMP funding, 91% local hiring, infrastructure contributions, genuine indigenous partnership — not afterthoughts but foundational deal terms

The MPP pathway combined with the MPSA structure and ACI's proven community model represents the lowest-risk, highest-alignment entry point for an Aboitiz partnership.
