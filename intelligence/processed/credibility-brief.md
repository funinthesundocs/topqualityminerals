### MGB ORDER — Official Government Approval of GMC as Operator

*Source: GMC DMC APPROVED MOA MGB_250306_084641.pdf — 8 pages (4 content + 4 blank), signed order from MGB Director*

This is **not a MOA** — it is an official **ORDER** issued by the Mines and Geosciences Bureau, signed by **Atty. Wilfredo G. Moncano, Director**, Quezon City.

**Subject:** Compromise Agreement, Mines Development and Operating Contract and Supplemental Contract Executed on February 04, 2022, February 23, 2017, and March 06, 2017, Respectively, by Dabawenyo Minerals Corporation and Genluiching Mining Corporation under MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI

**Key WHEREAS recitals (chronological history established by MGB):**
- July 28, 2007: DENR executed MPSA 251-2007-XI covering 6,917.09 hectares in Mati and Lupon, Davao Oriental
- July 31, 2008: Deed of Assignment (DOA) between DMC and Phil. Youbang Mining International Corporation assigned 1,010.42 hectares (MPSA split into 251A for DMC and 251B for Phil. Youbang)
- February 23, 2017: Mines Development and Operating Contract (MDOC) between DMC and GMC
- March 06, 2017: Supplemental Contract (SC)
- March 20, 2017: DMC Chairman Said J. Sayre, Sr. wrote to MGB RO No. XI requesting endorsement of cancellation of agreements with GMC
- May 30, 2017: DMC submitted Notice of Cancellation of the MDOC
- June 13, 2017: DMC Chairman Sayre wrote to MGB RO XI regarding final cancellation of the Feb 13, 2017 MDOC and March 06, 2017 SC
- **October 27, 2021**: MGB RO No. XI issued Memorandum to MGB Central Office recommending DMC show cause why MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI should not be cancelled for violations — entering multiple contracts allowing entities to conduct development/extraction without an approved DMPF
- **December 01, 2021**: MGB Central Office concurred — evaluation of MDOC and any agreement between DMC and other entities held in abeyance until DMC addressed violations
- **February 04, 2022**: DMC Board Resolution No. 12 (Series 2022) irrevocably re-instituted the Feb 13, 2017 MDOC and March 06, 2017 SC. Same day, DMC Board Resolution No. 13 cancelled, revoked, and nullified all deeds/contracts/agreements involving MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI entered through former Chairman/CEO Said J. Sayre, Sr., excluding the Feb 13, 2017 MDOC and March 06, 2017 SC
- **February 04, 2022**: Compromise Agreement (CA) executed between DMC and GMC
- **March 29, 2022**: MGB RO No. XI forwarded all documents to MGB Central Office for evaluation

**Key legal findings by MGB:**
- DENR Memorandum Order (DMO) No. 2004-09 provides that MOAs/Operating Agreements involving transfer/assignment of mining rights must be registered with MGB and subject to MGB Director approval
- DENR Administrative Order (DAO) No. 2021-37 provides that the MGB Director may allow one Operator within the Mining Tenement area, provided the Operator is a Qualified Person
- GMC is a Filipino corporation registered with SEC on June 22, 2012
- GMC meets eligibility requirements as a Qualified Person under DMO No. 99-10 and DAO No. 2010-21
- The Compromise Agreement, MDOC, and SC do not contravene pertinent provisions of RA No. 7942

**WHEREFORE — THE ORDER (6 conditions):**

The conduct of mining operations by GMC under MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI as **Operator** of Dabawenyo Minerals Corporation pursuant to the Compromise Agreement (Feb 4, 2022), MDOC (Feb 23, 2017), and SC (March 06, 2017) is hereby **approved**, subject to:

1. Activities shall be governed by RA 7942, its implementing rules, and the terms of MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI
2. **Failure to conduct mining operations for more than one year from issuance shall cause withdrawal of GMC's authority as Operator**
3. There is a solidary obligation — violation by either DMC or GMC of MPSA terms shall cause withdrawal of GMC's authority or termination/cancellation of MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI
4. Any disputes between DMC and GMC causing violation or non-compliance shall cause termination or cancellation of the MPSA
5. **This Order does not determine that the agreements are legal** — it only approves provisions authorizing GMC to undertake mining operations. All other provisions are internal to the parties and do not bind the Philippine Government
6. GMC shall within 30 days:
   a. Fully address and comply with the **TENEMENT (EXPLORATION) MONITORING CHECKLIST for CY 2020** issued by MGB RO No. XI
   b. File the application for renewal of the Exploration Period under MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI

**Signed by:** Atty. Wilfredo G. Moncano, Director, MGB (with official seal and QR code)

**Credibility Impact:** This is the **highest-authority document** in the entire GMC evidence set. It is a direct order from the MGB Director — the chief government authority over Philippine mining — explicitly approving GMC's operator status. The order also reveals the full chronological history of the DMC-GMC relationship including the 2017 conflict, the 2021 MGB show-cause threat against DMC, and the 2022 resolution. The fact that MGB Central Office reviewed all documents and issued a formal approval order (not just an acknowledgment) elevates GMC's legitimacy significantly.

### Concerns

3. LOI and copper contract are non-binding/unsigned templates

7. MPSA is "under exploration period" — not yet in production phase
8. Only partial DMPF provided (Sections 4-6, missing geological/resource sections)

### Overall Assessment
GMC is a **legitimate, SEC-registered mining company** with a valid MPSA covering nearly 6,000 hectares in Davao Oriental. The regulatory paperwork is in order. CCIC export certification (May 2015) confirms at least one commercial iron ore shipment to China at 64.71% Fe, proving the company has progressed beyond pure exploration. **Six independent laboratories** across four countries have now tested GMC material — an unprecedented breadth of independent validation for a Philippine exploration-stage company. Five completed drill holes with aggregate depth exceeding 177 meters demonstrate systematic subsurface exploration. MGB-filed quarterly exploration reports confirm active regulatory compliance. Diamond-level sponsorship at MAEM 2025, attendance at Australia-Philippines mining conferences with Ausenco, engagement of professional geological consultants (SGECS), and a 44-page international investor presentation (Singapore 2022) demonstrate active industry participation and institutional credibility. The DMPF financials should be treated as projections requiring independent verification, not as established performance.
