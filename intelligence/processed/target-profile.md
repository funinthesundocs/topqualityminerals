## 1. Strategic Priorities

ACI's stated priorities, extracted directly from their materials, ranked by emphasis:

### Vision Statement (explicitly stated)
> "We aspire to become the country's premier construction and asset management services provider by 2030."

### Massive Transformative Purpose
> "Building for Business to Prosper and for Communities to Thrive"

### Key Strategic Themes
1. **Vertical Integration** — They position as a "reliable single source" covering engineering, procurement, construction, AND asset management. They want to own the full lifecycle, not just build.
2. **Heavy Industry Dominance** — Power, oil & gas, chemical, cement, mining, logistics. This is not a residential builder. They play in industrial-scale infrastructure.
3. **Mining as a Core Competency** — Mining is listed as a primary service line. They have deep mining project history (Taganito HPAL, Red 5 Siana Gold, FCF Minerals gold/molybdenum, Abcar Paragon). Mining is NOT new to them.
4. **National Footprint with Southern Philippines Roots** — Cebu HQ, major yards in Balamban (Cebu) and Balingasag. Heavy project concentration in Visayas and Mindanao. Manila is secondary office.
5. **Asset Management / Maintenance Pivot** — Significant emphasis on maintenance services (power plants, cement plants, mining, airports, water treatment). They're moving beyond project-based construction into recurring revenue asset management.
6. **Safety as a Differentiator** — 25 million manhours without LTI. This is their proudest metric. Safety is identity, not compliance.

### CRITICAL INSIGHT FOR GMC
ACI's mission explicitly includes "drive sustainable growth for our clients, team members, and **host communities**." The community language is deliberate and aligns with humanitarian/legacy messaging in Section 7 of the presentation.

## 6. Partnership Evaluation Criteria (Inferred)

Based on what they emphasize in their own materials, ACI likely evaluates partners on:

### What They Care About
1. **Track record and proven history** — They lead with 50 years. They'll want to see GMC's geological proof, assay history, regulatory standing.
2. **Safety and compliance** — Their ISO certifications and safety metrics dominate the deck. Any partnership must demonstrate safety consciousness.
3. **Systematic operations** — PCAB, ISO, machine control technology. They're process-driven. GMC should present structured timelines, governance frameworks, not just opportunity.
4. **Community impact** — "Host communities" in their mission, MTP about "communities to thrive." They care about social license to operate.
5. **Long-term relationships** — Taganito HPAL spans 2011-2023+ (12+ years). They value enduring partnerships, not one-off projects.
6. **Scale and seriousness** — Their projects are 800+, spanning decades. They expect partners to be equally substantive.
7. **Regional alignment** — Heavy Visayas/Mindanao presence. GMC's Southern Philippines asset is geographically aligned with their operational footprint.

### Red Flags They'd Watch For
- Lack of regulatory compliance or licensing
- Unverified claims or inflated projections
- No track record or unproven operators
- Environmental negligence
- Disorganized governance or unclear structures

## 9. Presentation Strategy Implications

### What This Profile Tells Us About How to Present

1. **Lead with data, not narrative.** They're an engineering company. Geological assays, mineral composition data, financial projections with assumptions stated — this is their language.

2. **Mirror their "track record" framing.** Position GMC's geological history, regulatory milestones, and third-party validations as our track record.

3. **Emphasize the "single source" alignment.** Construction + mining = vertical integration. They already value being a single source provider. Show how GMC completes their capability chain.

4. **Name-drop their existing mining clients.** They already work with Taganito HPAL (nickel), Red 5 (gold), FCF Minerals. This makes mining partnership natural, not novel.

5. **Play the community card.** Their MTP is about communities thriving. GMC's humanitarian/community development angle should be prominent.

6. **Show governance and structure.** ISO certifications, PCAB — they respect formal systems. Present GMC's governance framework, regulatory compliance, corporate structure clearly.

7. **Use their visual language.** Big metric callouts, project photography, structured grids. Match their information density.

8. **Geographic narrative.** "We're both already in Southern Philippines. The infrastructure exists. The logistics are solved."

9. **Long-term partnership framing.** Their Taganito relationship is 12+ years. Frame GMC as a multi-decade partnership, not a project.

10. **Verify "Sabin" vs "Sabina."** The deck says "Sabin M. Aboitiz." Confirm exact name and gender before the meeting. Getting a decision-maker's name wrong is fatal.
