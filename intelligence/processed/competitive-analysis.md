## CRITICAL THREAT: NAC–DMCI MOU (March 2025)

Nickel Asia Corporation (NAC) — Aboitiz Construction's own THPAL client — signed an MOU with DMCI Mining in March 2025 to explore building a new nickel processing plant together. This is the single most important competitive development for our deal.

### Why NAC Chose DMCI Over Aboitiz

| What NAC Needed | What DMCI Brought | What Aboitiz Could Offer |
|----------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| Mining assets (ore supply) | Own nickel and gold tenements | Nothing — service contractor only |
| Construction capability | AAAA-rated D.M. Consunji Inc. | AAAA-rated Aboitiz Construction |
| Vertical integration | Mining + construction + power | Construction + maintenance only |

**The verdict**: DMCI was chosen because it brings mining assets PLUS construction capability. Aboitiz was passed over because it only brings construction services, not mining assets. This is the exact gap GMC fills.

### MOU Terms

- **NAC's role**: Nickel processing technology expertise, exploration capability, ore supply
- **DMCI's role**: New mining assets, construction and engineering capability
- **Timeline**: 2–3 years to evaluate technology, select site, secure ore supply
- **Target**: Process low-grade nickel ore not currently viable for export
- **Structure**: JV entity for development and operation; equity split TBD

### What This Validates

The NAC-DMCI deal validates exactly what we are proposing — a construction conglomerate partnering with a mining asset holder. The difference: we are bringing the proposal TO Aboitiz before a competitor locks them out. GMC is offering Aboitiz the mining asset contribution they need to compete with DMCI's vertically integrated model.

4. **CARAGA community credibility**: Top taxpayer in Claver for 3 consecutive years, ~1,000 local jobs, 91% local hiring, ₱13M+ annual SDMP. This social license is non-transferable.
5. **Sebastian Aboitiz's unique background**: BD manager who worked at the Norwegian partner of Aboitiz's hydropower JV and at a mineral processing site.

## US-PHILIPPINES CRITICAL MINERALS ALIGNMENT

A US-Philippines MOU on Critical Minerals Supply Chains was signed February 2026. The US specifically identifies opportunities in:
- Advanced mineral processing for nickel and copper (smelting, refining)
- Post-mine waste and tailings recovery
- Geological remote sensing and exploration technologies

The Philippines is pivoting away from China dependence (90% of nickel ore exports currently to China) toward diversified supply chains with the US, Japan, South Korea, EU. This geopolitical shift favors Western-aligned partners approaching Aboitiz.
