## 2. Mining Rights

### MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI

This is the core asset. A government-issued Mineral Production Sharing Agreement covering nearly 6,000 hectares of mineralized land in Davao Oriental.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Tenement** | MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI |
| **MPSA Holder** | Dabawenyo Minerals Corporation (DMC) |
| **Authorized Operator** | Genluiching Mining Corporation (approved May 2, 2022, MGB Central Office) |
| **Area** | 5,906.67 hectares |
| **Location** | Tarragona and Mati City, Davao Oriental, Region XI |
| **Commodities** | Molybdenum, copper, gold, and associated mineral deposits |
| **Date Approved** | July 28, 2007 |
| **Expiry** | July 28, 2032 (25-year initial term) |
| **Renewal** | Renewable for an additional 25 years (to 2057) |
| **Current Status** | Under exploration period (second renewal granted July 11, 2019) |

**What this means in practice.** An MPSA is the highest-tier mining agreement under Philippine law — issued by the national government through the Department of Environment and Natural Resources, verified and monitored by the Mines and Geosciences Bureau. It is not a permit application or a letter of intent. It is a vested contractual right to explore, develop, and extract minerals from a defined area for up to 50 years.

**Scale.** At 5,906.67 hectares, this tenement covers an area roughly 10 kilometers by 6 kilometers. The original concession area was 6,917.09 hectares, split in 2009 between DMC (5,906.67 ha as MPSA 251A) and PMIC (1,010.42 ha as MPSA 251B). GMC operates the larger portion.

**MGB Director's ORDER.** GMC's authorization as operator was not merely approved — it was formalized in a signed **ORDER** by MGB Director Atty. Wilfredo G. Moncano. The ORDER explicitly finds that GMC meets all eligibility requirements as a Qualified Person under DMO No. 99-10 and DAO No. 2010-21, that the Compromise Agreement and operating contracts do not contravene RA 7942, and formally approves "the conduct of mining operations by Genluiching Mining Corporation under MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI as Operator of Dabawenyo Minerals Corporation." This is the highest-authority government endorsement possible short of a production permit.

**SGECS Professional Report filed with MGB.** A comprehensive 30-page geological report by Sendon Geological and Environmental Consulting Services (May 2025) was formally submitted to and received by MGB Region XI (stamped received June 9, 2025). The report documents 8 completed drill holes with 493.1 meters aggregate depth, references results from 5 laboratories, and estimates 21.6 million MT of copper ore and 16-18 million MT of iron ore. Professional geological consulting of this caliber — with a licensed ACP geologist (PRC Reg. No. 5050097) as author — demonstrates institutional-grade exploration practice.

**Nine independent laboratories across five countries.** POSCO (Korea), Beijing BGRIMM (China), DALINC (Philippines), Intertek (Philippines), HK Imperial (Philippines), Ostrea Mineral Labs (Philippines), CCIC Philippines (Chinese government-affiliated), SGS Korea (Busan Branch), and Laboratorium Uji Kimia Dan Mekanik (Indonesia) have all tested GMC material. This breadth of independent validation is unprecedented for a Philippine exploration-stage company.

## 3. Operating Relationship

### GMC as Authorized Operator under DMC

GMC does not hold the MPSA directly. It operates under a contractual arrangement with Dabawenyo Minerals Corporation, the tenement holder. This structure is standard in Philippine mining — it separates the rights holder from the operating company that brings capital, equipment, and technical capability.

**Contract history:**

| Date | Agreement | Key Terms |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| February 23, 2017 | Original Operating Contract | DMC assigns GMC full and exclusive authority to develop MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI. GMC assumes all compliance obligations and expenses. DMC receives 15% of net income. Term co-terminus with MPSA. |
| March 6, 2017 | Supplemental Contract | GMC advances P5,000,000 total to DMC (deductible from DMC's 15% share). Legal venue changed from Makati to Davao City. |
| February 4, 2022 | **Compromise Agreement** (supersedes above) | Revised revenue split: **20% of total net income to DMC** (up from 15%). GMC to provide 2 Nissan Terra 4x4 vehicles upon DENR approval. Monthly sustenance allowance of P200,000 to DMC (non-deductible). GMC released P5,000,000 to DMC. Validity: 11 years, renewable. |

### Addressing the Prior Conflict — Transparently

Between the 2017 contracts and the 2022 Compromise Agreement, there was a period of dispute between DMC and GMC. The record shows:

- GMC filed Criminal Case NPS Docket No. INV-215-03844 against the DMC Board
- DMC issued cancellation letters and cease-and-desist orders against GMC

This is not hidden. It is resolved. The February 2022 Compromise Agreement formally re-instituted all prior contracts, withdrew the criminal case, nullified the cancellation letters, and established revised terms that both parties endorsed. All documents are registered with MGB Regional Office XI in Davao City, with documentary stamps.

**The honest read:** Operator-holder disputes are common in Philippine mining. What matters is how they resolve. In this case, both parties returned to the table, restructured terms (DMC received a higher revenue share, monthly sustenance, and upfront cash), and re-committed to the partnership with MGB endorsement. The relationship has been formally stable since February 2022 — over four years. But any prospective partner should be aware of this history and factor it into relationship risk assessment.

## 8. What's Honestly Missing

A credible presentation acknowledges what is not yet in place. Aboitiz will conduct rigorous due diligence — they have institutional processes for this. Anything we obscure will surface during their review and damage trust. Better to frame these ourselves, accurately, and position the partnership as the mechanism that addresses them.

### Pre-Production Status
GMC has not generated mining revenue. No ore has been commercially extracted, processed, and shipped under the current operating arrangement. The company is pre-production. The MPSA has been in its exploration period since 2007 — 18 years without DMPF approval or transition to production phase. This is the central fact that any investor or partner will identify first.

**Our framing:** GMC is an early-stage company with proven assets — a valid MPSA, MGB-endorsed operator status, regulatory compliance, equipment investment, and commercial interest from international buyers. What it lacks is the capital partner and infrastructure capability to move from exploration to production. That is precisely the role Aboitiz Construction would fill.

### LOI and Contract Status
The Hubei Global Union LOI is explicitly non-binding. The copper contract is an unsigned template with a blank seller field. These demonstrate commercial interest and market pricing, but they are not enforceable commitments and should not be presented as confirmed offtake agreements.

### Aboitiz MOU
The existing MOU template in GMC's files references Aboitiz Construction, Inc. — but it is a blank, unexecuted document concerning wind power projects, not mining. It is not an active agreement. It should not be referenced in the presentation as evidence of an existing relationship. It demonstrates awareness and interest, nothing more.

### Operational Scale at Site
The Mati site currently has approximately 6 employees. This is an exploration-stage staffing level, not a production operation. The DMPF plans for 141 positions, but that workforce does not yet exist.

### DMPF Projections
The financial projections (78% IRR, P13.4B NPV, 2.44-year payback) are model outputs from 2021, not audited results. The timeline they modeled (2023-2033) has already slipped. These numbers should be referenced as feasibility-study projections requiring independent verification — never as established returns.

### Family-Controlled Entity
GMC is controlled by the Tupas and Mendiola families. All original incorporators share the same residential address in Tanza, Cavite. This is common in Philippine mining companies but will be noted by any institutional partner conducting governance review.

## Presentation Guidance

### For Section 1 (Opening — Establish Credibility)
Lead with the concrete, verifiable facts: SEC registration since 2012, Makati CBD headquarters, MGB-endorsed operator status over nearly 6,000 hectares, 20x capital increase, seven active permits all current. These are not claims — they are documented, cross-referenced, and independently verifiable through SEC and MGB records. Let the regulatory paper trail do the credibility work.

### For Section 4 (The Proof)
Layer the evidence: mining rights (government-issued, MGB-verified) first, then operating relationship (resolved and re-endorsed), then commercial momentum (LOI, contract terms, export registrations, equipment investment), then DMPF projections (with appropriate caveats). End with the honest-gaps section framed as "this is where the partnership begins." The gaps are not weaknesses to hide — they are the investment opportunity. If GMC were already in production with confirmed offtake, it would not need a strategic partner. The partnership proposition exists precisely because there is work to be done, and the foundation to do it from is solid.

### Tone Calibration
Aboitiz Construction built and has maintained the Taganito HPAL nickel processing plant in Claver, Surigao del Norte since 2012 — a 13-year operational relationship with nearly 1,000 local jobs and 10 million safe man-hours. They understand mining partnerships. They understand that projects begin before production. What they will evaluate is whether the foundation is real (it is), whether the team is transparent (we must be), and whether the opportunity justifies their capital and capability (the MPSA, the geology, and the market demand say yes).

Do not oversell. Do not hide gaps. Present GMC as what it is: a legitimate, early-stage mining company with proven regulatory assets, active commercial positioning, and a clear need for the exact capabilities Aboitiz Construction brings. The honesty is the credibility.
