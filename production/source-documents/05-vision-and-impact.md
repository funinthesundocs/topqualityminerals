## 1. Beyond the Deal -- A Shared Purpose

This partnership is not measured only in tonnage and revenue. It is measured in communities transformed, families lifted, and a nation's resources converted into lasting prosperity.

Aboitiz Construction's Massive Transformative Purpose -- "Building for Business to Prosper and for Communities to Thrive" -- is not a slogan on a slide deck. It is a commitment tested over fifty years and proven in places like Claver, Surigao del Norte, where a construction contract became a 13-year community institution. Genluiching Mining Corporation's vision -- "Bringing Precious Ores to the Light -- To Prosper Lives" -- carries the same conviction: that what lies beneath the ground has no value unless it creates value above it.

These are not two companies searching for alignment. These are two organizations that already share a purpose: build something that lasts, and make sure the people closest to it benefit first.

The partnership proposed here is the mechanism through which that shared purpose becomes tangible -- in jobs created, in families sustained, in infrastructure that reaches communities the market has overlooked, and in a national minerals strategy that keeps value in Filipino hands.

## 2. The Davao Oriental Transformation

The Mati concession sits in one of the Philippines' most resource-rich but economically underserved regions. Davao Oriental's eastern seaboard communities depend on subsistence agriculture, small-scale fishing, and informal livelihood. The infrastructure gap -- roads, reliable power, telecommunications, potable water -- keeps these communities disconnected from the economic corridors of Davao City and beyond.

A mineral processing operation changes this trajectory. Here is what the partnership creates:

### Direct Employment

The Declaration of Mining Project Feasibility projects a minimum of 141 permanent positions at the processing facility -- all Filipino, with local preference built into every hiring tier. These are not seasonal or contract roles. They are full-time, skilled positions with the training and career progression that transforms a household's economic future from precarious to stable.

### Indirect Employment

For every direct job at a mineral processing facility, multiple indirect positions emerge across the supply chain: equipment suppliers, transport operators, food services, hospitality, maintenance subcontractors, fuel distribution, safety equipment providers, and professional services including legal, accounting, and environmental monitoring. The economic multiplier in underserved regions is substantial because these services do not yet exist at scale -- the operation creates the local economy, not just a workplace within it.

### The Claver Model -- Replicated

Aboitiz Construction already knows what this looks like. In Claver, Surigao del Norte, the THPAL engagement created over 1,000 local jobs, made ACI a top business taxpayer for three consecutive years, and built a social license so deep that the company's leadership -- President, Executive Director, and a fifth-generation Aboitiz family member -- visited the site together in January 2026. That is not a client relationship. That is an institution.

The Davao Oriental project replicates this model in a new region with the same fundamentals: a mineral-rich concession, an underserved host community, and a partnership structure designed for decades, not quarters. The question is not whether the Claver model works. It is proven. The question is where to apply it next.

### Infrastructure That Reaches Underserved Barangays

Mining development requires roads, power, water, and telecommunications. These assets do not stop at the facility gate. In Claver, the infrastructure built to serve THPAL connected surrounding barangays to services they had never accessed. In Davao Oriental, the same dynamic applies: roads engineered for heavy haul become the roads that connect farming communities to markets. Power infrastructure extended to the processing site becomes the backbone for rural electrification. Telecommunications towers installed for operational coordination bring connectivity to households that have never had it.

This is not charity. It is the natural byproduct of industrial development done at scale and done well. AboitizPower, Aboitiz InfraCapital, and ACI already operate in adjacent sectors -- power generation, economic estates, water treatment, airport management. The ecosystem to deliver this infrastructure already exists within the Aboitiz Group. The missing piece is the specific project that activates it.

### Skills Development

GMC's current workforce includes mining laborers, road mappers, guides, stockyard personnel, and property custodians -- many drawn from subsistence livelihoods with no prior industrial experience. A formal mineral processing operation introduces structured training programs: equipment operation, safety protocols, quality control, environmental monitoring, and maintenance skills aligned with ISO standards. These programs transition subsistence workers into skilled industrial professionals whose capabilities are portable and career-defining.

ACI's own workforce development track record -- 91% local hiring, 4,000+ skilled team members deployed nationwide (as of October 2025, per Stevie Awards submission), three consecutive years as a Best Employer -- provides the institutional knowledge to design and deliver these programs. The partnership does not just hire people. It builds the skilled workforce that Davao Oriental has never had.

## 3. Indigenous Partnership -- The Lumad Community

The Lumad peoples of Davao Oriental are not an obstacle to be managed. They are a partner to be elevated.

GMC already employs a workforce that is approximately 60% Lumad. These are not token positions. Lumad community members serve as mining laborers, road mappers and guides who know the terrain better than any surveyor, stockyard personnel, and property custodians whose stewardship of the land predates the concession itself. This is an existing relationship built on daily trust, shared labor, and mutual dependence.

The partnership deepens this relationship through GMC's planned Foundation, designed specifically for the Lumad and other catch-basin communities. The Foundation will provide:

- **Literacy education** -- bringing formal educational access to communities where it has been limited or absent
- **Livelihood training** -- equipping community members with skills in agriculture, trades, and enterprise management beyond mining
- **Enterprise creation and marketing** -- supporting Lumad-led businesses that can sustain themselves independently of the mining operation
- **Socialized housing** -- replacing informal structures with dignified, permanent homes
- **Primary health care** -- delivering basic medical services to communities that currently depend on distant municipal facilities

This is not a Social Development and Management Programme created to satisfy a regulatory checkbox. It is a genuine investment in the autonomy and prosperity of the people who live on and care for this land.

### FPIC as Partnership, Not Process

Free, Prior, and Informed Consent is the single most important permit in Philippine mining -- and the one most likely to fail when it is treated as a procedural hurdle. Genuine consent comes from genuine benefit. When the indigenous community sees itself reflected in the workforce, in the foundation's programs, in the housing and health care and education that the operation delivers, consent is not extracted. It is offered -- because the community recognizes the partnership as its own.

The THPAL model in Claver demonstrates how this works in practice: sustained community investment that predates and outlasts any single project phase, delivered consistently enough to build trust that cannot be replicated by a competitor arriving with a checklist and a budget line.

## 4. Environmental Stewardship

Responsible development is not an add-on to this partnership. It is embedded in the operating model.

### Processing, Not Extraction

The strategic framing of this partnership -- mineral processing and downstream beneficiation rather than raw extraction and export -- carries inherent environmental advantages. Processing adds value to ore already extracted under an existing MPSA, reducing the total volume of material that must be moved, transported, and managed. The environmental footprint per unit of economic value is fundamentally smaller when you process domestically rather than ship raw ore abroad.

### Reforestation and Wildlife Protection

GMC operates a forest tree nursery at the Mati site, tended by the Lumad community, to reforest land affected by mining activities. Wild pine, hardwood, and fruit-bearing seedlings are carefully transplanted and propagated in alternative green areas. Road signs protect wildlife. These are not gestures -- they are standing programs with community participation and institutional continuity.

### Progressive Reclamation

Philippine mining law requires progressive reclamation -- the rehabilitation of mined land as operations advance, not after they conclude. The partnership will implement progressive reclamation plans that meet or exceed DENR requirements, ensuring that the land is returned to productive use on a rolling basis rather than left as a remediation liability for future generations.

### Standards and Compliance

The processing facility will operate under ISO 14001 environmental management standards -- the same certification that Aboitiz Construction already holds. Mercury management plans, dust suppression systems, water management protocols, and waste handling procedures will be designed to international standards and audited regularly. ACI's ISO 14001 certification is not aspirational; it is current. The operational systems already exist.

### Dry Processing Technology

GMC's procurement of HCG-1230 dry drum magnetic separators -- equipment that processes iron ore without water -- reflects a deliberate choice to minimize environmental impact. Dry magnetic separation eliminates water usage in the beneficiation stage, avoids the creation of wet tailings, and reduces the facility's water management burden. This is environmentally responsible technology selected before any partnership pressure demanded it.

## 5. The National Vision

President Marcos has been direct about what the Philippines needs from its mineral wealth:

> "We want to go beyond just the phase of extracting the minerals and to actually go vertically integrate that entire activity all the way down to actual battery production."

This partnership answers the President's call.

### Critical Minerals for the Energy Transition

The Mati concession contains copper -- the mineral that underpins every electric vehicle, every charging station, every power grid upgrade, and every renewable energy installation that the global energy transition requires. The International Energy Agency projects that copper demand for clean energy technologies will double by 2040. The Philippines holds significant copper resources but currently exports them as raw ore, capturing only a fraction of the value chain.

A domestic mineral processing operation -- copper beneficiation, iron ore concentration, gold and silver recovery -- keeps that value in the Philippines. It creates Filipino jobs, generates Filipino tax revenue, builds Filipino industrial capability, and positions the nation as a participant in the critical minerals supply chain rather than a raw material source.

### The US-Philippines Critical Minerals MOU

In February 2026 -- weeks ago -- the United States and the Philippines signed a Memorandum of Understanding on critical minerals supply chain cooperation. This is not historical policy. It is current national priority. The MOU positions the Philippines as a strategic partner in the West's effort to diversify mineral sourcing away from concentrated supply chains. A Filipino-led, Filipino-majority mineral processing operation is precisely the kind of investment this framework was designed to support.

### Iron for National Industrialization

Iron ore grading above 65% Fe -- premium grade confirmed by POSCO, the world's sixth-largest steelmaker -- feeds the Philippines' construction, infrastructure, and industrial sectors. Every bridge, every building, every port expansion that the nation's infrastructure program demands requires steel. Domestic iron ore processing reduces import dependence and strengthens the industrial base.

### The Fiscal Framework Supports This

Republic Act 12253, signed by President Marcos in September 2025, established the first stable fiscal framework for Philippine mining. The President framed it explicitly: the wealth from mineral resources should fund national development. A partnership that processes minerals domestically, employs Filipinos, pays taxes locally, and builds infrastructure in underserved regions is precisely what this law was designed to incentivize.

## 6. The Aboitiz Legacy

The Aboitiz family has spent a century building a conglomerate across six sectors: power, banking and financial services, food and beverage, infrastructure, real estate, and data and technology. Each expansion was a generational decision -- a bet placed not for quarterly returns but for the decades that followed.

Sabin Aboitiz's Great Transformation is the current generation's defining move: a seven-year, 380-billion-peso capital deployment program to transform a family conglomerate into the Philippines' first techglomerate. The results speak: 45% revenue growth, 500% increase in awarded contracts at ACI, a 105-billion-peso capital expenditure budget in 2025 alone, strategic partnerships with BlackRock, JERA, and Coca-Cola. The Great Transformation is not a plan. It is underway.

But look at the portfolio and one gap is conspicuous. Every other major Philippine conglomerate touches natural resources -- San Miguel in cement and quarrying, DMCI in nickel and coal, Ayala in energy and EV manufacturing. Aboitiz has construction services for mining clients. It does not own, operate, or invest in mineral processing itself.

The platform for mineral processing already exists within the Aboitiz ecosystem. ACI builds processing plants -- proven at THPAL and the 583-million-peso copper ore sorting facility for CD Processing. ACI Plus maintains them -- proven across 13 years at THPAL. AboitizPower supplies the energy. Aboitiz InfraCapital provides the infrastructure. Aboitiz Data Innovation brings the data science and predictive maintenance technology. Every capability is in place. The only missing node is the specific mineral processing asset.

This partnership fills that gap. It adds a seventh sector to the Aboitiz portfolio -- natural resources -- and it does so through the lowest-risk entry point available: mineral processing, not extraction. Processing leverages every existing Aboitiz capability. It aligns with the techglomerate thesis. It generates the recurring revenue that the Great Transformation is designed to capture. And it embeds the Aboitiz name in the Philippines' critical minerals story at the moment that story is being written.

A century from now, the decisions that defined the Aboitiz Group will include the Great Transformation. This partnership is where that transformation meets the ground.

## 7. The Human Metric

There is a metric that does not appear on any balance sheet but defines whether a partnership has mattered.

For every ton of ore processed at the Mati facility, measure not just the revenue generated but the families fed by the 141 direct jobs and the hundreds of indirect positions that follow. Measure the children who attend school because their parents hold stable employment for the first time. Measure the Lumad families housed through the Foundation, the communities connected to roads and power and telecommunications that did not exist before this partnership. Measure the young workers from Davao Oriental who entered as laborers and emerged as skilled professionals with careers that outlast any single mine.

President Marcos, signing the Enhanced Fiscal Regime into law, stated the standard clearly:

> "The wealth from our soil should transform into schools for our children, hospitals for our families, and roads that connect our people with opportunities."

This partnership is built to meet that standard. Not through aspiration, but through mechanism: a Foundation with defined programs, a workforce with local preference, infrastructure that extends beyond the facility gate, and a partnership structure designed to operate for decades.

The Aboitiz Group's MTP says it plainly: "Building for Business to Prosper and for Communities to Thrive." GMC's vision says the same: "Bringing Precious Ores to the Light -- To Prosper Lives."

This is the shared purpose. This is the partnership. This is what it builds.
