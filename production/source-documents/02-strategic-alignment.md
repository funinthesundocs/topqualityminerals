## 1. The Thesis

This is not a pitch. It is a recognition that Aboitiz Construction has already built — over fifty years and eight hundred projects — the exact capabilities, relationships, geographic presence, and strategic direction that make this partnership inevitable. ACI constructed and has maintained the Philippines' most significant nickel processing facility for thirteen consecutive years. It built a ₱583 million copper ore sorting plant. It operates across Southern Philippines with 91% local hiring and deep community trust. Its parent conglomerate has deployed ₱380 billion across six sectors in the Great Transformation — every sector except the one where ACI already has proven expertise. The Aboitiz ecosystem already contains every capability needed for mineral processing: construction, maintenance, power, infrastructure, data science, and financial backing. The question is not whether Aboitiz should enter mineral processing. The question is when, and with whom.

## 2. Seven Points of Alignment

### 2a. The Build-Then-Maintain Model

GMC is the next THPAL.

Aboitiz Construction's most significant strategic evolution is the formalization of its build-then-maintain model — the playbook proven at the Taganito HPAL facility, where ACI built the $1.7 billion nickel processing plant in 2012 and has maintained it continuously for thirteen years. That single relationship anchored the launch of ACI Plus (formerly IMS) as a dedicated business segment, generating ₱650 million in revenue in 2024 with a target of ₱1 billion by 2026.

The GMC mineral processing facility represents precisely the kind of anchor client ACI Plus needs to reach that target. A purpose-built processing plant requires years of construction — heavy industries work that is core to ACI's PCAB Quadruple A capability — followed by decades of maintenance: preventive, predictive, shutdown, mechanical, instrumentation, and electrical. The same lifecycle. The same revenue model. The same strategic logic that transformed a one-time construction contract at Claver into a thirteen-year relationship and a ₱650 million business segment.

ACI's Vision 2030 — "the country's premier construction and asset management service provider" — depends on securing new long-duration maintenance clients. GMC offers a multi-decade engagement that serves both sides of that vision simultaneously.

### 2b. The ₱583M Copper Plant Precedent

This is not theoretical. Aboitiz Construction has already built a mineral processing facility.

In September 2021, ACI completed a ₱583 million copper ore sorting plant for CD Processing Ltd. at Carmen Copper Corporation in Toledo City, Cebu. The facility uses Sandvik magnetic resonance analyzer technology, 16X belt conveyors, and structural modules — a state-of-the-art mineral processing operation that received BOI pioneer project approval. CD Processing, a British company, obtained a Mineral Processing Permit and sources run-of-mine ore from the existing mining operation. ACI built the plant. The model is established.

The GMC partnership follows the same architecture at greater scale: a processing facility built by ACI, maintained by ACI Plus, with ore sourced under agreement from an existing MPSA holder. The difference is scope, duration, and the strategic significance of having the Aboitiz ecosystem — not just the construction arm — behind the operation.

### 2c. Southern Philippines Footprint

GMC's 5,906-hectare mining asset in Davao Oriental sits squarely within Aboitiz Construction's operational heartland.

ACI is headquartered in Cebu with its primary industrial facility — a 44,630-square-meter assembly yard with deepwater pier — at West Cebu Industrial Park in Balamban. Its project history spans Surigao del Norte (THPAL, Red 5 Siana Gold), Misamis Oriental (Balingasag yard), Davao, General Santos, and Bukidnon. The company knows the terrain, the logistics corridors, the regulatory environment, the community dynamics, and the labor markets of Southern Philippines. This is not an expansion into unfamiliar territory. It is an extension of existing operations into an adjacent geography.

The same regulatory bodies (DENR Region XI, MGB-XI), the same indigenous peoples' frameworks (NCIP), the same provincial and municipal governance structures, the same transportation networks. For a company that has operated in Surigao del Norte for over a decade, Davao Oriental is the next province over — not the next frontier.

### 2d. The Great Transformation's Missing Piece

The Aboitiz Group's Great Transformation has deployed ₱380 billion across six sectors since 2020: power, banking and financial services, food and beverage, infrastructure, real estate, and data/technology. The ₱105 billion capex budget for 2025 alone — a 37% increase over the prior year — reflects a conglomerate in aggressive deployment mode, seeking the right opportunities to place capital at scale.

Every other major Philippine conglomerate has a presence in natural resources. San Miguel operates the country's largest cement and quarry operations. DMCI Holdings runs nickel and copper mining alongside Semirara coal. Ayala has moved into energy transition and EV manufacturing. The Lopez Group has natural gas through First Gen. Aboitiz, with over a century of history and six diversified sectors, has never held a mining asset. The construction arm has served mining clients for decades, but the Group has no equity position in the resource sector.

This is the conspicuous gap. Not a weakness — an unrealized opportunity. The capabilities are already in place across the ecosystem. What has been absent is the specific opportunity that fits the Group's risk profile, aligns with the techglomerate thesis, and can be championed by the leadership team already engaged with mining clients.

### 2e. The Ecosystem Platform

No other potential partner can deploy the full Aboitiz ecosystem against a mineral processing opportunity. The capabilities needed already exist — not as aspirations, but as operating businesses with proven track records:

**Aboitiz Construction (ACI)** builds the processing facility. 800+ completed projects. PCAB Quadruple A — one of only 72 contractors in the Philippines with no project cost ceiling. Proven at THPAL ($1.7B facility), CD Processing (₱583M copper plant), and across mining, power, petrochemical, and cement sectors.

**ACI Plus** maintains the facility for decades. ₱650 million in revenue and growing. Thirteen years of continuous maintenance at THPAL. Preventive, predictive, shutdown, and corrective maintenance. EquiMax digital monitoring deployed, delivering 30% improvement in machine control efficiency.

**AboitizPower** supplies energy. 9,200 MW target portfolio spanning renewable, LNG, hydro, and battery storage. A mineral processing plant requires reliable, large-scale power — AboitizPower is the country's leading private power producer.

**Aboitiz InfraCapital (AIC)** provides supporting infrastructure. Airports, economic estates, water systems (Apo Agua), telecom towers. Site access, water supply, and logistics infrastructure are within existing competence.

**Aboitiz Data Innovation (ADI)** brings data science and AI optimization. EquiMax predictive maintenance is already deployed at THPAL. Process optimization, yield analysis, and remote monitoring capabilities align mineral processing with the techglomerate vision.

**UnionBank** and the AEV balance sheet provide financial capacity. BlackRock/GIP's ₱13.7 billion investment in Aboitiz InfraCapital in 2025 signals global institutional confidence in the Group's investment decisions.

Every node in this network already exists. The only missing element is the mineral processing asset itself.

### 2f. Community and Social License

Aboitiz Construction's Massive Transformative Purpose — "Building for Business to Prosper and for Communities to Thrive" — is not a tagline. It is an operational practice documented across thirteen years in Claver, Surigao del Norte.

At the THPAL facility, ACI created approximately 1,000 local jobs. It maintains a 91% local hiring rate across all operations. It has been recognized as a top business taxpayer in the Municipality of Claver for three or more consecutive years. Its community programs include Brigada Eskwela educational support, tree planting and environmental rehabilitation, and disaster preparedness initiatives. Twenty-seven million safe man-hours without a lost-time injury (as of October 2025, per Stevie Awards submission). These are not CSR line items — they are the foundation of a social license to operate that took years to build and that competitors cannot replicate.

GMC's asset in Davao Oriental presents an analogous community context. The operation employs a workforce that is approximately 60% Lumad (indigenous), with plans for a community foundation focused on education, healthcare, and livelihood development. The alignment is structural: both organizations define success not only through operational and financial performance but through measurable community impact. ACI's proven model at Claver — genuine, sustained, community-first investment that predates and outlasts any single project phase — is precisely the model that earns and sustains an FPIC endorsement and LGU support in indigenous communities.

### 2g. Processing, Not Extraction

The Mineral Processing Permit (MPP) pathway allows entry into mineral processing without holding mining rights or undertaking extraction. A separate Philippine-incorporated entity obtains the MPP from MGB, sources ore from existing MPSA holders under supply agreements, and builds and operates the processing facility. This is the model CD Processing Ltd. used at Carmen Copper — and the model that aligns with every stated Aboitiz priority.

For a technology-forward conglomerate pursuing the techglomerate thesis, mineral processing is the right entry point into the resource sector:

- **Technology-driven.** Processing plants use advanced sorting, leaching, floatation, and refining technologies. Sandvik magnetic resonance analyzers. HPAL chemistry. Predictive maintenance via EquiMax. This is engineering, not excavation.
- **Data-enabled.** Process optimization, yield monitoring, quality control, and supply chain management are data science problems — the domain of Aboitiz Data Innovation.
- **Recurring revenue.** A processing facility generates continuous revenue from throughput, not one-time project fees. This is the annuity-style earnings model the Great Transformation is designed to build.
- **Manageable risk profile.** No greenfield exploration uncertainty. Processing operates on proven technology with contracted ore supply and established offtake markets. The risk profile is closer to manufacturing than to speculative mining.
- **Nation-building alignment.** President Marcos has stated the national ambition clearly: "We want to go beyond just the phase of extracting the minerals and to actually go vertically integrate that entire activity all the way down to actual battery production." A major Filipino conglomerate entering mineral processing is a direct response to that call.

The distinction matters. This is not a proposal for Aboitiz to become a mining company. It is a proposal for the Aboitiz ecosystem to do what it already does — build, maintain, power, connect, optimize, and finance industrial operations — in the mineral processing sector.

Consider the requirements for a mineral processing partnership in Southern Mindanao:

- **Construction capability at scale.** PCAB Quadruple A — one of 72 contractors in the Philippines qualified for projects of any size. Heavy industries specialization across power, petrochemical, cement, and mining. 800+ completed projects.
- **Maintenance business model.** A dedicated, growing industrial maintenance segment (ACI Plus) designed to provide decades of post-construction service. Not a bolt-on — a strategic growth vector targeting ₱1 billion in revenue.
- **Mineral processing track record.** The ₱583M copper ore sorting plant at Toledo City. Thirteen years of continuous maintenance at THPAL. Mining listed as a core competency, not an adjacent capability.
- **Southern Philippines operational presence.** Cebu headquarters, Balamban industrial yard, active projects across Surigao, Davao, General Santos, Misamis Oriental. Established relationships with regional regulators, LGUs, and communities.
- **Conglomerate ecosystem.** Power generation, infrastructure development, data science and AI, financial services — every supporting capability available within one corporate group.
- **Community track record.** 91% local hiring, top taxpayer status in mining communities, 27 million safe man-hours (as of October 2025, per Stevie Awards submission), documented social development programs. A social license to operate that has been earned, not claimed.

No other potential partner combines all six. Smaller regional contractors lack the PCAB qualification and financial capacity.

The alignment between what GMC's mineral processing opportunity requires and what Aboitiz Construction uniquely provides is not incremental. It is categorical.

## 5. The Policy Moment

The Philippine regulatory and investment landscape for mineral processing has not been this favorable in fifteen years — and the window is open now.

**RA 12253 — Enhanced Fiscal Regime for Large-Scale Metallic Mining (September 2025).** The first unified, predictable tax framework in Philippine mining history. The Chamber of Mines endorsed it as establishing "a stable, transparent, and competitive fiscal environment." Margin-based royalties, clear excise tax structures, and defined LGU revenue sharing replace the ad hoc regime that deterred institutional investment for decades.

**Executive Order 130 and the Open-Pit Mining Ban Reversal (2021).** The nine-year moratorium on new mineral agreements was lifted. The blanket prohibition on open-pit operations was removed. The regulatory posture shifted from restriction to managed development.

**The US-Philippines Critical Minerals MOU (February 2026).** Signed weeks ago. Establishes bilateral cooperation on critical minerals supply chains — positioning the Philippines as a strategic source of nickel, copper, cobalt, and chromite for Western markets seeking alternatives to Chinese and Indonesian processing dominance.

**BOI Pioneer Status for Mineral Processing.** The Board of Investments grants pioneer project status — with associated fiscal incentives — to qualifying mineral processing investments. CD Processing Ltd. received this designation for its ₱583M copper ore sorting plant. A larger-scale, Filipino-led processing operation would be a strong candidate.

**President Marcos's Stated Priority.** The President has articulated the national vision with unusual specificity: "We want to go beyond just the phase of extracting the minerals and to actually go vertically integrate that entire activity all the way down to actual battery production." A major Filipino conglomerate entering mineral processing is not merely aligned with government policy — it is a direct answer to the President's call.

The convergence is rare. Fiscal clarity, regulatory openness, bilateral strategic alignment, investment incentives, and explicit presidential direction — all pointing toward mineral processing as a national priority. For a conglomerate already deploying ₱105 billion in annual capital expenditure and actively seeking the next frontier of the Great Transformation, the timing is not coincidental. It is structural.

A partnership between Aboitiz and GMC in mineral processing positions the Aboitiz Group not only as a participant in this policy moment but as the Filipino conglomerate that answered the call — with the construction capability, the maintenance model, the energy supply, the technology platform, and the community credibility to execute.
