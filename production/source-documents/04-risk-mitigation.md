## 1. Our Approach to Risk

We believe the best way to build trust is to address concerns before they are raised.

Companies that hide risks are not partners — they are liabilities. What follows is a complete inventory of every significant risk we have identified in this opportunity, assessed honestly and paired with a specific mitigation strategy. Where a risk is serious, we say so. Where data is incomplete, we say that too.

Aboitiz Construction operates to ISO-certified engineering standards. You evaluate partnerships the way you evaluate structures — load by load, joint by joint, with zero tolerance for hidden weakness. This document is written in that spirit: every risk identified, every mitigation defined, every residual exposure acknowledged.

The result is not a risk-free opportunity. No mining project is. The result is a risk-managed opportunity where the unknowns are bounded, the path to resolution is clear, and the cost of finding answers is modest relative to the potential value.

## 3. The Due Diligence Commitment

We welcome and encourage Aboitiz's due diligence process. We do not merely tolerate scrutiny — we seek it. Independent verification of every material claim strengthens the partnership foundation and protects both parties.

**Recommended due diligence scope:**

| Workstream | Scope | Purpose |
|------------|-------|---------|
| **Independent geological assessment** | JORC- or NI 43-101-compliant resource estimation by a qualified person from a recognized firm. Includes verification of all assay data, core logging, geological modeling, and resource classification. | Replaces internal estimates with bankable resource numbers. |
| **Legal review** | Full review of MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI, the DMC-GMC Compromise Agreement, operating contracts, LGU endorsements, and all permit conditions. Title chain and encumbrance search. | Confirms the legal foundation is sound and identifies any exposure. |
| **Environmental baseline study** | Pre-disturbance environmental conditions including water quality, biodiversity, air quality, and mercury baseline levels. Conducted by an independent environmental consultancy. | Establishes the baseline against which all future environmental performance is measured. Required for ECC application. |
| **Community consultation** | Independent assessment of indigenous community sentiment, ancestral domain claims, existing relationships, and FPIC readiness. Conducted by specialists with Lumad engagement experience. | Provides an honest reading of community support before FPIC process begins. |
| **Financial audit** | Review of GMC's audited financial statements, asset verification (including the P316M existing assets claimed in the DMPF), capital structure, and obligations. | Confirms the financial position of the partner entity. |

We will facilitate full access to all documents, personnel, sites, and records. Any item requested that we cannot provide, we will say so directly and explain why.

The cost of comprehensive due diligence on a project of this nature is measured in the low millions of pesos. The potential value of a confirmed porphyry copper-gold system in a 5,906-hectare concession, with production-grade iron ore at 67.31% Fe (POSCO-confirmed) and near-concentrate surface copper at 39.5% Cu (four-lab corroborated), is measured in billions. The asymmetry favors action.

## 4. Why the Risk-Reward Ratio Favors Action

Every risk in the matrix above is real. None has been minimized. Here is what sits on the other side of the ledger:

**The regulatory window is the most favorable in 15 years.** Three landmark policy shifts — EO 130 lifting the mineral agreement moratorium, the open-pit mining ban reversal, and RA 12253 establishing a predictable fiscal regime — have converged within a five-year span. This window may narrow after the 2028 election. The companies that move now, while the framework is clear and the government is actively encouraging investment, will secure the strongest positions.

**Nine independent laboratories across five countries and three geophysical methods converge on the same deposit model.** POSCO confirmed production-grade iron at 67.31% Fe. Intertek's 136-sample campaign proved deposit-scale mineralization across the concession. Beijing BGRIMM corroborated copper-gold grades through CNAS-accredited arbitration testing. Davao Analytical confirmed near-concentrate copper at 39.5% Cu and grade consistency across a decade of sampling. HK Imperial demonstrated commercial processing viability at 120,000 WMT scale. Ostrea Mineral Labs confirmed 20.35 g/t Au — the highest gold result ever recorded from the concession — and 63.84% Fe from drill core, with 7-year grade consistency (2017-2024). CCIC Philippines independently confirmed 59.34% Fe iron and 6.33% Cu copper from drill core. SGS Korea and an Indonesian lab provided the 8th and 9th independent confirmations. This is convergent evidence from institutions across five countries with no relationship to each other, each answering a different geological question, all arriving at the same conclusion. A professional 30-page geological report by SGECS (May 2025), formally filed with MGB Region XI, synthesizes all findings, documents 8 drill holes with 493.1 meters aggregate depth, and estimates 21.6 million metric tons of copper ore within less than 9% of the concession.

**The geological data confirms massive upside at depth — and critical permitting risks have been retired.**  FPIC has been approved. The DMC court ruling is final. The MGB Director has personally endorsed GMC's operator status. The MPSA extension is in principle granted. Four of the five highest-risk items in this matrix have been resolved or substantially de-risked.

**The geophysical data confirms the porphyry model.** Electromagnetic resistivity surveys identify conductive anomalies at 80 to 200 meters depth, structurally aligned with surface mineralization, consistent with a porphyry copper-gold system. The inclusion of molybdenum in the original MPSA commodity list — a hallmark porphyry indicator — suggests this potential was recognized from the earliest geological assessment. If the porphyry model is confirmed by drilling, the project value increases by an order of magnitude.

**The entry pathway manages the highest risks.** The Mineral Processing Permit model allows Aboitiz to enter through a processing facility — the exact model used at THPAL in Claver, where Aboitiz Construction built and has maintained the plant for over a decade. This avoids the extraction-phase environmental, community, and reputational risks while establishing the infrastructure and relationships needed for long-term development.

**The cost of finding answers is minimal compared to the potential value.** A drill program of 5 to 10 holes, an independent feasibility study, environmental baseline work, and legal due diligence represent a fraction of the capital required to develop the project. These investments resolve the key uncertainties — copper at depth, mercury management requirements, FPIC readiness, resource classification — and provide the data needed for a fully informed investment decision.

A 5,906-hectare concession in the Pacific Ring of Fire, where nine independent laboratories across five countries and three geophysical methods converge on a polymetallic deposit model — production-grade iron, near-concentrate copper, gold at 20.35 g/t Au, and 21.6 million metric tons of estimated copper ore within less than 9% of the concession. Eight drill holes, 493.1 meters aggregate depth, a professional geological report filed with the national mining regulator. FPIC approved. Court ruling final. MGB Director's ORDER confirming operator status. MPSA extension in principle granted. An existing MPSA, an established community relationship, and a regulatory environment actively encouraging development. This is not a speculative greenfield prospect. It is an advanced exploration project with a defined path to production, where the critical permitting and legal risks have already been resolved, offered at the moment when every external condition favors moving forward.

The risks are manageable. The opportunity is substantial. The next step is due diligence.
