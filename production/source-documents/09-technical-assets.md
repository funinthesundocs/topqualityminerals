## 1. Executive Summary of the Asset

Genluiching Mining Corporation holds MPSA 251(A)-2007-XI, a 5,906-hectare mineral concession in Mati City and Lupon, Davao Oriental — one of the larger tenements among the 287 valid MPSAs in the Philippines. The concession hosts confirmed iron ore, copper, and gold mineralization validated by six independent laboratories across four countries. Surface iron ore has been confirmed at grades up to 67.31% Fe — a premium product that exceeds global shipping benchmarks. Copper grades up to 39.5% Cu and gold at up to 4.4 g/t Au have been independently verified. A geophysical survey covering the concession has identified conductive anomalies at 80-200 meters depth, consistent with a porphyry copper-gold system — the deposit type that supplies the majority of the world's copper. Forty-four hectares of confirmed surface mineralization have been GPS-mapped within the concession, with geological models suggesting substantially more mineral wealth at depth. The asset is drill-ready for the next phase of systematic development.

## 3. Grade Highlights

### Iron Ore: Up to 67.31% Fe

- **Result:** 67.31% Fe in lump ore, 64.11% Fe in fines (POSCO, South Korea)
- **Corroborated by:** 56.06% Fe (Intertek, 136 samples), 53.4% Fe (Davao Analytical, 2025), 61.8% Fe (Davao Analytical, 2012)
- **Global benchmark:** The international shipping standard for iron ore is 62% Fe. Ore above this threshold trades at premium prices on global commodity markets. GMC's lump ore grade of 67.31% Fe places it among the highest-quality iron ore products globally — comparable to top-tier Brazilian and Australian operations.
- **Financial implication:** Premium-grade iron ore (65%+ Fe) commands a price premium of USD $5-15 per ton over the 62% Fe benchmark. At scale, this premium alone can represent tens of millions of dollars in additional annual revenue.
- **Quality indicators:** Very low phosphorus (0.012-0.021%) and sulfur (0.002-0.013%) — both well below penalty thresholds for steelmaking feedstock.

### Copper: Up to 39.5% Cu

- **Result:** 39.5% Cu in surface copper ore sample (Davao Analytical, February 2025)
- **Corroborated by:** 28.1% Cu (Davao Analytical, 2012), 15.13-20.72% Cu (Beijing BGRIMM/CIQ), 18.02% Cu in processed concentrate (HK Imperial)
- **Context:** Typical copper mines operate economically at grades of 0.5-1.0% Cu. A surface sample at 39.5% Cu represents near-concentrate grade material — essentially, ore so rich it approaches the output grade of a processing plant. Even the lower corroborating results (15-28% Cu) are exceptionally high by global standards.
- **Financial implication:** At current copper prices (~USD $9,000-10,000 per tonne), high-grade copper zones represent substantial value. The consistency of high copper grades across four independent labs confirms this is not an anomaly — it is a systematic feature of the deposit.

### Gold: Up to 4.4 g/t Au

- **Result:** 4.4 g/t Au (Davao Analytical, January 2025)
- **Corroborated by:** Multiple gold detections across sampling campaigns (0.2-4.4 g/t Au in recent assays; up to ~15.07 g/t Au in Beijing BGRIMM concentrate analyses)
- **Context:** Open-pit gold mines typically operate profitably at grades above 1.0 g/t Au. Underground mines can be economic at 3-5 g/t Au. At 4.4 g/t Au, the gold component alone is commercially significant — and it occurs as a co-product alongside copper and iron, meaning gold revenue supplements the primary commodity streams.
- **Financial implication:** Gold is a hedge asset. In a multi-commodity operation, gold by-product credits can offset operating costs, improving the economics of the entire project.

### Additional Value: Silver, Zinc, Molybdenum

- **Silver:** Up to 382.95 g/t Ag in copper concentrate (HK Imperial processing data). Silver adds meaningful by-product revenue.
- **Zinc:** 10.31-12.55% Zn in concentrate (HK Imperial). A secondary revenue stream from the copper processing circuit.
- **Molybdenum:** Listed on the MPSA as a concession commodity. Molybdenum is a hallmark mineral of porphyry copper deposits and commands premium pricing for steel alloy applications. Not yet assayed separately — represents untapped upside.

## 4. Geological Model — Porphyry Copper-Gold System

### What the Geophysical Survey Revealed

A comprehensive electromagnetic (EM) survey deployed three independent geophysical methods across the concession:

1. **3D Geoelectric Resistivity** — mapped the subsurface structure to hundreds of meters depth, revealing a pipe-like hydrothermal conduit beneath the concession. This vertical structure is consistent with the plumbing system of a large mineral deposit, where hot, metal-rich fluids rose from deep in the earth's crust and deposited minerals as they cooled.

2. **Induced Polarization (IP)** — detected zones of disseminated sulfide minerals (the type of minerals that host copper and gold) at depth. IP anomalies correspond directly with the resistivity anomalies, providing independent confirmation.

3. **VLF Electromagnetic** — mapped conductive structures to 100-200 meters depth. Multiple linear conductive anomalies were detected, aligned with the same northwest-southeast fault systems identified in surface geological mapping. Cross-method validation between VLF Line 9 and geoelectric resistivity Lines 3 and 4 confirmed the same subsurface features through two independent techniques.

### What This Means in Plain Language

The three geophysical methods independently identified the same features: conductive anomalies at 80-200 meters depth, connected to a pipe-like structure extending deeper into the earth. This pattern is the signature of a **porphyry copper-gold deposit** — the deposit type that produces approximately 75% of the world's copper, 50% of its molybdenum, and significant quantities of gold.

The geophysical report explicitly interprets these anomalies as "characteristic of an epithermal or porphyry copper-gold system," consistent with the concession's position within the Philippine volcanic arc — one of the world's most prolific porphyry copper belts.

### Why Porphyry Deposits Matter

Porphyry deposits are the preferred targets of major mining companies worldwide because they combine three qualities: large tonnage, long mine life, and multi-commodity revenue. A confirmed porphyry system typically supports mining operations lasting 30-50+ years. The world's largest copper mines — Escondida (Chile), Grasberg (Indonesia), Oyu Tolgoi (Mongolia) — are all porphyry deposits.

The Philippines hosts several proven porphyry systems, including the Tampakan copper-gold deposit in South Cotabato (one of the largest undeveloped copper-gold deposits globally) and the operating mines in the Surigao-CARAGA mineral belt where Aboitiz Construction has direct operational experience through the Taganito HPAL project.

## 5. Surface to Subsurface Correlation

The strength of the GMC asset lies not in any single data point, but in the coherent story told by multiple independent methods from surface to depth.

### Layer 1: Surface (0 meters) — Confirmed by Geological Mapping

Eleven GPS-located iron ore observation points spanning 800m x 550m, documented by licensed geologist Medilyn Gonzaga. Surface exposures range from 0.5m to 14m in size, with ore described as moderately to highly magnetic. Iron ore occurs along a northwest-southeast structural corridor at contacts between basalt, dacite, and metasedimentary rock units — a pattern consistent with structurally controlled mineralization.

Four independent laboratories confirmed surface iron grades of 53.4-67.31% Fe. Four laboratories confirmed surface copper grades of 1.3-39.5% Cu. Gold detected at 0.2-4.4 g/t Au across multiple sampling campaigns from 2012 to 2025.

### Layer 2: Shallow Subsurface (0-28 meters) — Confirmed by Diamond Drilling

Diamond drill hole DH5-SBF1-A (HQ3 core, 28 meters total depth) confirmed iron oxide mineralization from 2 meters to 24 meters depth. Peak mineralization occurred at 16-20 meters: 40% hematite, 25% manganese, and 10% limonite — the strongest iron interval in the hole. Mineralization is fracture-controlled and hosted within a propylitic alteration envelope (epidote, chlorite, silica), which is the characteristic alteration halo surrounding porphyry copper systems.

The drill hole confirmed that surface iron grades are not superficial — mineralization continues to at least 24 meters depth with increasing intensity. Argillic (clay) alteration at the base of the hole (27-28 meters) indicates proximity to a deeper hydrothermal system.

### Layer 3: Intermediate Depth (80-200 meters) — Confirmed by Geophysical Survey

Three independent geophysical methods (resistivity, IP, VLF) identified conductive anomalies at 80-200 meters depth. These conductive zones are consistent with sulfide mineralization — the type of minerals that host copper, gold, and molybdenum at depth. The anomalies are aligned with the same northwest-southeast fault systems that control surface mineralization, indicating a single, continuous mineral system from surface to depth.

### Layer 4: Deep Structure (200+ meters) — Indicated by 3D Resistivity Model

The 3D resistivity inversion reveals vertical columnar structures extending below 200 meters, interpreted as the roots of the hydrothermal system — the feeder conduits through which mineral-rich fluids reached the near-surface ore bodies. A pipe-like anomalous structure consistent with a deep-seated magmatic-hydrothermal source has been identified. This is the geological engine that created the deposit.

### The Coherent Story

Iron oxides at surface and in drill core represent the oxidized cap of a larger sulfide system at depth. High copper grades at surface represent enriched material above sulfide outcrops. Conductive anomalies at 80-200 meters represent the main sulfide ore body — the primary target. The pipe-like deep structure represents the source. Every layer of data confirms the same geological model: a porphyry copper-gold system with iron-rich surface expression, validated from the surface down through four independent methods.

## 6. The 11 Iron Ore Observation Points

Licensed geologist Medilyn Gonzaga documented eleven GPS-located iron ore exposures across the concession, spanning an area of approximately 800 meters (northwest-southeast) by 550 meters (northeast-southwest) — a confirmed mineralization footprint of approximately 44 hectares within the 5,906-hectare concession.

| Cluster | Points | Area | Key Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ridge cluster** (NW-SE ridge) | Points 1-4 | ~126deg13'50"-56" E | Iron ore lenses within metasedimentary rock; exposures up to 10m x 14m; pyritic and magnetic |
| **Creek/dacite cluster** (SE creek) | Points 6-10 | ~126deg14'03"-10" E | Structurally controlled by S76degE fault; iron in basalt and dacite; moderately to strongly magnetic |
| **Extension points** | Points 5, 11 | Outliers 300-500m from main clusters | Point 5: highly pyritic, highly magnetic; Point 11: 2m x 12m exposure. These extend the known mineralization well beyond the core area |

Ore descriptions consistently note: yellowish brown to brownish black coloration, highly pyritic content, moderate to high magnetic response, and structural orientations between N11degE and N18degE. The presence of acid leaching textures at multiple points indicates supergene enrichment — a natural geological process that has concentrated and upgraded the near-surface ore over geological time.

**In business terms:** Visible mineral wealth is exposed at the surface across a substantial area. These are not isolated occurrences — they form a systematic pattern controlled by the same geological structures, indicating a large, connected mineral system beneath the surface. The 44 hectares of confirmed surface mineralization represent less than 1% of the total concession area.

## 9. Risk Disclosures

Transparency strengthens credibility. The following items require acknowledgment and management planning:

### Mercury Contamination

Samples from the February 2025 Davao Analytical batch returned mercury levels of 1,245 mg/kg (copper ore) and 1,429 mg/kg (iron ore). These levels are significant and require specialized processing protocols, strict compliance with the Minamata Convention on Mercury, and a dedicated environmental management plan. Mercury handling is a solvable engineering challenge, but it must be addressed proactively in any feasibility study.

### Anomalous Iron Result Requires Verification

One iron ore sample (M-25-455) returned 85.4% Fe — a reading that exceeds the theoretical maximum for pure magnetite (72.4% Fe). This result is almost certainly from a beneficiated or concentrated product, not run-of-mine ore, or represents an analytical misread. It should not be cited as representative of in-situ ore grades. Independent verification through re-sampling and re-assay is recommended.

### Copper Zone Not Yet Drilled

While surface copper grades of 1.3-39.5% Cu have been confirmed by four independent laboratories, and geophysical anomalies at 80-200 meters depth are consistent with copper sulfide mineralization, no drill hole has yet intersected the copper zone at depth. The single completed drill hole (28 meters) tested only the iron zone. Confirmation of copper at depth requires targeted drilling — the priority next step.

### Resource Estimate Status

A JORC Code or NI 43-101 compliant resource estimate has not yet been completed. Current tonnage figures (18.2 million to 292.5 million tons in internal geological reports) are exploration targets and conceptual estimates only — they were not prepared to international reporting standards and should not be relied upon for investment decisions without further work. Completing a compliant resource estimate is a defined milestone in the development pathway, achievable once the targeted drilling program is complete.

### Geological Complexity

The deposit shows clear zonation — iron and copper mineralization occupy different spatial zones within the concession. The drill hole that confirmed iron oxides to 24 meters depth recorded no copper minerals. This spatial separation is consistent with the porphyry deposit model (iron oxides form an oxidized cap above copper sulfides at depth), but it means that iron and copper operations may require separate mining fronts and processing circuits.
