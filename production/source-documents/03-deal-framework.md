## 1. Recommended Structure: MPSA with Filipino-Majority JV

The Philippine Mining Act provides three mineral agreement types. For this partnership, one structure is clearly superior.

**Why MPSA, not FTAA:**

| Factor | MPSA | FTAA |
|--------|------|------|
| Filipino ownership requirement | 60% minimum | None (100% foreign allowed) |
| Government revenue split | Production share (predictable) | **60% government / 40% contractor** on net revenue |
| Term | 25 + 25 years (50 total) | 25 + 25 years |
| Approval authority | DENR Secretary | President of the Philippines |
| Track record | Hundreds issued | **Only 5 FTAAs ever issued** |

The FTAA imposes a punitive 60-40 revenue split favoring the government — designed for projects where no Filipino entity can provide the capital or technology. That is not this partnership. With Aboitiz as the Filipino-majority partner, the 60% ownership threshold is met naturally, and the MPSA pathway avoids the FTAA's fiscal penalty entirely.

The 25+25 year MPSA term provides multi-generational security — a 50-year horizon that matches Aboitiz's century-long institutional planning culture and ACI's demonstrated appetite for decade-long engagements (13+ years at THPAL and counting).

**The existing MPSA — MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI — is already in place**, held by Dabawenyo Minerals Corporation (DMC), covering 5,906.67 hectares in Tarragona and Mati City, Davao Oriental. GMC is the authorized operator, approved by MGB Central Office in May 2022. The mineral agreement does not need to be obtained — it needs to be developed.

## 2. The MPP Pathway — Strategic Entry Point

The most significant structural insight in this proposal is the **Mineral Processing Permit (MPP)** pathway. This is not mining. It is mineral processing — and the distinction matters enormously for risk, reputation, and returns.

### How It Works

A separate Philippine-incorporated entity:

1. **Obtains a Mineral Processing Permit** from MGB (a distinct permit from the MPSA)
2. **Sources ore from existing MPSA holders** (GMC/DMC) under negotiated supply agreements
3. **Builds and operates the mineral processing plant** — construction by ACI, maintenance by ACI Plus
4. **Does NOT hold mining rights** and does NOT undertake extraction — avoiding the political, environmental, and reputational risks associated with mining itself

This is downstream beneficiation. The new entity processes raw ore into higher-value concentrate or refined product, then sells to international offtakers. The extraction risk stays with the MPSA holder. The processing entity captures value-add margins with a fundamentally different risk profile.

### Why This Matters for Aboitiz

Aboitiz has never owned a mining asset in 100+ years. Every other major Philippine conglomerate touches mining directly — San Miguel, DMCI, Ayala. The conspicuous absence is not accidental; it reflects a deliberate posture on reputational risk.

The MPP pathway resolves this. Mineral processing is:

- **Aligned with the techglomerate thesis** — technology-driven, data-enabled, demonstrably modern
- **Aligned with national policy** — President Marcos has stated explicitly: "We want to go beyond just the phase of extracting the minerals and to actually go vertically integrate that entire activity all the way down to actual battery production"
- **Aligned with the US-Philippines Critical Minerals MOU** signed February 2026
- **Eligible for BOI pioneer project status** — tax incentives for qualifying investments

### The Precedent: CD Processing Ltd.

This is not a theoretical structure. ACI has already executed it.

**CD Processing Ltd.** — a British company — obtained BOI pioneer project approval for a **₱583 million copper ore sorting plant** at Carmen Copper Corporation in Toledo City, Cebu. The facility uses Sandvik magnetic resonance analyzer technology with 16X belt conveyors and structural modules. It sources run-of-mine ore from the existing Carmen Copper mining operation under a supply agreement. **Aboitiz Construction built the facility**, completing it in September 2021.

The model we are proposing is the model ACI has already built. The only difference is scale — and partnership.

### MPP Application Requirements

| Requirement | Status / Path |
|-------------|---------------|
| Application form (MGB Form No. 11-02) | Standard filing |
| Feasibility study with plant design, technology, anti-pollution measures | To be developed jointly |
| Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC) | EMB application (6-12 months) |
| NCIP Certification Precondition (if in indigenous area) | Required — FPIC process |
| LGU endorsement | Provincial, municipal, barangay |
| Proof of financial capability (audited financials, credit lines) | Aboitiz balance sheet resolves this |
| Supply contract with ore source (MPSA holder) | GMC/DMC supply agreement |
| Five-year activity history of applicant | New JV entity — principals provide track record |

## 3. Proposed Partnership Architecture

The partnership is structured as an ecosystem, not a single entity. Each Aboitiz business unit contributes its core competency. Each revenue stream is distinct and contractually defined.

### Entity Map

```
DABAWENYO MINERALS CORPORATION (DMC)
│   MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI holder
│   5,906.67 hectares — Tarragona & Mati City, Davao Oriental
│   Commodities: Molybdenum, Copper, Gold, associated minerals
│
├── GENLUICHING MINING CORPORATION (GMC)
│       Authorized operator (MGB-approved May 2022)
│       Exploration, extraction, MPSA compliance
│       Revenue: Operations minus 20% net income to DMC
│
└── NEW JV ENTITY (Filipino-majority, Aboitiz-led)
        MPP holder — Mineral Processing
        Processes ore sourced from GMC/DMC under supply agreement
        Builds, owns, and operates processing plant
        │
        ├── ACI — Construction Contractor
        │     Builds the processing facility
        │     Earns: Construction contract fees
        │
        ├── ACI Plus — Maintenance Contractor
        │     Long-term industrial maintenance (build-then-maintain model)
        │     Earns: Recurring maintenance fees (multi-decade)
        │
        ├── AboitizPower — Energy Supply
        │     Power supply agreements for processing operations
        │     Earns: Energy supply revenue
        │
        ├── Aboitiz InfraCapital (AIC) — Infrastructure
        │     Roads, port access, water systems, site development
        │     Earns: Infrastructure development + service fees
        │
        └── Aboitiz Data Innovation (ADI) — Technology
              EquiMax monitoring, process optimization, data analytics
              Earns: Technology licensing + service fees
```

### Revenue Flows

| Entity | Revenue Source | Nature |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| DMC | 20% of total net income from GMC mining operations | Royalty / production share |
| GMC | Mining operations revenue minus DMC share and operating costs | Extraction operations |
| New JV Entity | Processed mineral sales to international offtakers (minus ore supply costs, processing costs, taxes) | Processing margin |
| ACI | Construction contract for processing plant | One-time project fee |
| ACI Plus | Multi-decade maintenance contract | Recurring annual fees |
| AboitizPower | Energy supply to processing operations | Utility revenue |
| AIC | Infrastructure development and services | Development + service fees |
| ADI | Technology deployment and optimization | Licensing + service revenue |

### What This Means for Aboitiz

Every Aboitiz business unit earns revenue from this partnership. ACI earns a major construction contract. ACI Plus earns decades of maintenance revenue — contributing directly to its ₱1 billion IMS revenue target. AboitizPower supplies energy. AIC develops infrastructure. ADI deploys technology. The JV entity itself generates processing margins.

This is not a single transaction. It is an ecosystem deployment — exactly the "single source" integration model ACI positions as its competitive advantage.

## 4. Fiscal Regime (RA 12253)

Republic Act 12253 — the "Enhanced Fiscal Regime for Large-Scale Metallic Mining Act" — was signed by President Marcos on September 4, 2025. It establishes the most predictable fiscal environment Philippine mining has ever had.

### Tax and Revenue Structure

| Component | Rate / Detail |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Royalty** (inside mineral reservations) | 5% of gross output |
| **Royalty** (outside mineral reservations) | Margin-based royalty on income |
| **Windfall tax** | Applied when profit margins exceed 30% |
| **Excise tax** | 4% on minerals (retained from TRAIN Act 2017) |
| **LGU share** | 40% of gross collections from excise taxes, royalties, and related charges |
| **MGB/research allocation** | 10% of royalties directed to exploration, research, environmental protection |
| **Taxable entity** | Each mining project treated as a separate taxable entity — no cross-project loss offsetting |
| **Audit authority** | BIR and Bureau of Customs authorized to audit mineral sales and exports |

### Why This Matters

The Chamber of Mines of the Philippines endorsed RA 12253 as establishing a "stable, transparent, and competitive fiscal environment." For decades, Philippine mining suffered from an unpredictable patchwork of national and local taxes, executive orders, and ad hoc revenue demands. RA 12253 replaces ambiguity with a codified framework.

For a process-driven organization like Aboitiz — ISO triple-certified, systematic in governance — fiscal predictability is a prerequisite, not a bonus. This law provides it.

The separate taxable entity provision is particularly relevant: the MPP-holding JV entity would be taxed independently from GMC's extraction operations, providing clean financial separation and transparent accounting for each partner's returns.

## 5. Governance Framework

The partnership governance structure is designed to mirror the systematic, certification-driven standards Aboitiz applies across its operations.

### Board Composition

- **Board seats proportional to equity contribution** in the JV entity
- Independent director(s) with mining industry or financial oversight credentials
- Annual board elections per Philippine corporate governance code
- Regular board meetings (minimum quarterly) with formal agenda and minutes

### Technical Committee

| Function | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| Geology & Resource | Drill program oversight, resource estimation review, reserve classification |
| Processing & Engineering | Plant design review, technology selection, operational optimization |
| Environmental & Compliance | ECC compliance, EPEP monitoring, progressive rehabilitation oversight |
| Safety | Zero-LTI target, safety management system aligned with ISO 45001 |

### Community Advisory Board

- **Indigenous peoples' representation** — formal mechanism for ongoing FPIC engagement beyond the statutory requirement
- Local government unit liaison
- Community development program oversight (SDMP: 1.5% of operating cost per regulation)
- Environmental monitoring participation
- Modeled on the THPAL community engagement approach in Claver: 1,000 local jobs, 91% local hiring, sustained investment that predates operations

### Audit and Reporting

| Mechanism | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Independent financial audit | Annual |
| Operational performance reporting | Quarterly |
| Environmental compliance reporting | Quarterly (to DENR-EMB) |
| Safety reporting | Monthly |
| Community development reporting | Semi-annual |
| Board reporting package | Quarterly |

### Standards Alignment

The governance framework is designed to be compatible with:

- **ISO 9001** (Quality Management)
- **ISO 14001** (Environmental Management)
- **ISO 45001** (Occupational Health & Safety)

ACI holds all three certifications. The JV entity's governance should meet or exceed the same standards from inception — not as an aspiration, but as a founding condition.

## 6. Phased Development Timeline

The timeline below reflects realistic assessment of Philippine mining permit processes, informed by precedent projects (Silangan: 10+ years to production; Celsius MCB: first new copper MPSA in 15 years; DENR target of 2-year permitting with digital reforms).

### Phase 1: Foundation (Year 1)

| Activity | Detail | Owner |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Due diligence | Independent geological review, legal audit, financial verification | Joint |
| Site visit | Physical inspection of MPSA area, Mati City operations | Joint |
| JV formation | Philippine-incorporated entity, equity structure, shareholder agreement | Legal counsel |
| MPP application | MGB Form 11-02 filing, initial requirements assembly | JV entity |
| Drill program | Target EM anomalies identified in existing geophysical surveys | GMC + technical consultant |
| Supply agreement | Ore supply terms between GMC/DMC and JV entity | Legal counsel |

### Phase 2: Development Planning (Years 1-2)

| Activity | Detail | Owner |
|----------|--------|-------|

| ECC application | Environmental Impact Statement submission to EMB-DENR | Environmental consultant |
| FPIC process initiation | Engagement with NCIP and indigenous communities | JV entity + NCIP |
| Plant design | Processing technology selection, engineering design, cost estimation | ACI + process engineers |
| Offtake exploration | Preliminary discussions with international mineral buyers | JV entity |
| LGU engagement | Provincial, municipal, barangay endorsement process | JV entity + community team |

### Phase 3: Construction (Years 2-4)

| Activity | Detail | Owner |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Permitting completion | ECC, FPIC, LGU endorsements, MPP approval | JV entity |
| Construction | Processing plant construction by ACI | ACI |
| Infrastructure | Access roads, port facilities, water systems, power connection | AIC + AboitizPower |
| Offtake agreements | Binding contracts with international buyers | JV entity |
| Technology deployment | EquiMax monitoring, process control systems | ADI |
| Community programs | SDMP implementation, local hiring, training programs | JV entity + ACI |

### Phase 4: Operations (Year 4+)

| Activity | Detail | Owner |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Operations commence | Plant commissioning, first processed mineral output | JV entity |
| ACI Plus maintenance begins | Long-term industrial maintenance contract activates | ACI Plus |
| Offtake fulfillment | Contracted shipments to international buyers | JV entity |
| Community programs scale | Full SDMP funding (1.5% of operating cost), expanded local employment | JV entity |
| Continuous optimization | ADI data analytics, process efficiency improvements | ADI |

### Critical Path Item: FPIC — APPROVED

**Free and Prior Informed Consent has been secured.** This was previously identified as the longest lead time and highest risk permit in the sequence. FPIC approval has been obtained through the NCIP process, reflecting GMC's longstanding relationship with the indigenous community — the workforce is approximately 60% Lumad.

The approval retires the single most significant permitting bottleneck. Ongoing community engagement remains essential — the THPAL model of sustained investment and local hiring (91% local workforce, top taxpayer, community programs) is the template for maintaining social license throughout operations. FPIC approval is a milestone, not an endpoint.

### Next Commercial Milestone: First Vessel Shipment

The immediate operational milestone is **shipping one vessel of ore for the commercial mining permit**. This is the transition from exploration to commercial production — the threshold that activates the full revenue model. GMC has the BOC exporter registration (February 2026), PHILEXPORT membership, and magnetic separator equipment (USD 180K, ordered January 2026) in place. The partner's role accelerates this timeline.

The Declaration of Mine Project Feasibility filed with MGB provides the following capital framework:

| Stockholder advances | ₱93.3M | 6.12% |

### DMPF Financial Projections (2021)

| Metric | Projected Value |
|--------|----------------|
| IRR (Base Case) | 78.26% |
| Payback period | 2.44 years |
| NPV | ₱13.36 billion |
| Mine life | 11 years (modeled 2023-2033) |
| Unit operating cost | US$31.63/MT |
| Break-even selling price | US$37.14/MT |
| Equity structure assumed | 60% Filipino / 40% Foreign |

### Important Caveats

These are 2021 projections prepared for a 2023 operational start that has not materialized. They require updating through an **independent, bankable feasibility study** as part of Phase 2. Specifically:

1. **Cost escalation** — Construction, equipment, and labor costs have changed since 2021
2. **Commodity price assumptions** — Copper, gold, and molybdenum prices require current market modeling
3. **RA 12253 fiscal impact** — The new tax regime (signed September 2025) was not reflected in the 2021 DMPF
4. **Processing plant scope** — The MPP-based processing entity may have a different capital profile than the integrated mining-and-processing model in the original DMPF
5. **Independent verification** — The 78% IRR and ₱13.4B NPV projections are optimistic and unverified by a third party

The DMPF provides a useful starting framework for understanding project scale and financing structure. It does not substitute for the updated independent study that any serious partnership requires. We are transparent about this because Aboitiz will expect nothing less.

## 8. GMC's Position on Partnership Terms

GMC's leadership has indicated expectations for the JV equity structure:

- **Preferred split: 60/40 or 70/30** — with Aboitiz as the majority partner providing capital, construction capability, and operational discipline
- **DMC receives 20% of net profit** from mining operations — this is fixed under the Compromise Agreement and is non-negotiable
- **GMC controls the site, the documents, and the relationships** — the MPSA, MGB approvals, community consent (FPIC), indigenous workforce relationships, and geological knowledge reside with GMC
- **The JV partner enters under GMC's existing rights** — the MPSA is already in place, MGB Director's ORDER already issued, FPIC already approved, drill data already collected

**The framing from GMC's principal: "The principles are already agreed. This meeting is orientation to what's inside the mountain."**

This is not a negotiation meeting. The commercial framework — JV structure, DMC royalty, GMC's role as operator — is established. What remains is for the partner to understand the asset, verify the data through due diligence, and decide whether to commit capital and capability to development.

## 9. The Ask

This meeting is not a request for a signed agreement. It is a proposal for a structured next step.

### What We Are Proposing

A mutual commitment to proceed with:

1. **Due diligence** — Independent review of geological data, legal standing, corporate structure, and regulatory status
2. **Site visit** — Physical inspection of the MPSA area in Tarragona and Mati City, Davao Oriental
3. **Joint feasibility assessment** — Scoping study to determine whether a full bankable feasibility study is warranted

### What This Requires

- A small, defined commitment of time and resources from both parties
- No equity commitment at this stage
- No binding obligation beyond the due diligence process itself
- A clear decision gate: at the conclusion of due diligence, both parties independently assess whether to proceed to the feasibility study phase

### Why This Structure

The next step is small and reversible. The opportunity is not.

MPSA No. 251(A)-2007-XI expires July 28, 2032, with a 25-year renewal option. The regulatory window — EO 130 lifting the moratorium, the open-pit ban reversal, RA 12253 establishing fiscal predictability — is the most favorable in 15 years. The US-Philippines Critical Minerals MOU was signed three weeks ago. The policy environment is aligned, the asset is real, and the partnership architecture leverages every major Aboitiz business unit.

What we are asking for is the opportunity to prove, through rigorous due diligence, that this partnership merits the next phase of commitment. The data will speak for itself — or it will not. Either outcome is valuable. But the only outcome that serves neither party is inaction while the window remains open.
