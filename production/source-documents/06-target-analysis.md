## 4. Partnership Evaluation Framework

Based on what ACI emphasizes in their own materials, they will evaluate GMC against these criteria:

### What They Look For

1. **Track record and proven history.** They lead with 50 years. They will want to see GMC's geological proof, assay history, regulatory standing, and any prior operational track record. Third-party validations (LOIs, MOUs, independent assays) carry significant weight.

2. **Safety and compliance.** ISO certifications and safety metrics dominate their corporate deck. Any partnership must demonstrate safety consciousness. GMC should present whatever safety protocols, environmental compliance, and regulatory approvals exist.

3. **Systematic operations.** PCAB, ISO, machine control technology — they are process-driven. GMC should present structured timelines, governance frameworks, corporate structure. Disorganized presentations or vague plans will trigger rejection.

4. **Community impact.** "Host communities" in their mission, MTP about "communities to thrive." They care deeply about social license to operate. GMC's community development narrative should be prominent and specific.

5. **Long-term relationships.** The Taganito HPAL relationship spans 2012 to present (13+ years). They value enduring partnerships. Frame GMC as a multi-decade relationship — never as a project.

6. **Scale and seriousness.** 800+ projects spanning decades. They expect partners to be equally substantive. Evidence of scale, commitment, and investment readiness is essential.

7. **Regional alignment.** Heavy Visayas/Mindanao presence. GMC's Southern Philippines asset is geographically aligned with their operational footprint. This is a major advantage — emphasize it.

### Red Flags They Will Watch For

- Lack of regulatory compliance or licensing
- Unverified claims or inflated projections
- No track record or unproven operators
- Environmental negligence or indifference
- Disorganized governance or unclear corporate structures
- Purely financial pitches without operational substance
- Vague timelines or missing governance frameworks

## 6. The Great Transformation Context

### The GT in Brief

- **Launched:** 2020 by CEO Sabin Aboitiz
- **Scale:** ₱380 billion (~$7B) seven-year capital deployment program
- **Catalyst:** COVID crashed power demand, exposing dangerous revenue concentration
- **Thesis:** Transform a 100-year-old conglomerate into the Philippines' first "techglomerate"

### Three Pillars

1. **Portfolio diversification** — enter new sectors to reduce power dependence
2. **Technology integration** — deploy data science/AI across all units via Aboitiz Data Innovation (ADI)
3. **Culture transformation** — instill startup culture in a century-old family conglomerate

### Six Current Sectors

Power | Banking (UnionBank) | Food & Beverage (Coca-Cola PH) | Infrastructure (airports, towers, data centers) | Real Estate | Data/Technology

**NO MINING.** This is the most conspicuous absence. Every other major Philippine conglomerate touches mining or resources (San Miguel, DMCI, Ayala, Lopez). Aboitiz has never owned a mining asset in 100+ years.

### The Missing Piece

The gap between "Aboitiz has never been in mining" and "Aboitiz enters mineral processing" is far smaller than it appears:

| Capability | Already Exists Via |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Build processing plants | ACI (THPAL, ₱583M copper plant) |
| Maintain processing plants | ACI Plus / IMS (THPAL 13+ years, Republic Cement) |
| Supply energy | AboitizPower (9,200 MW target) |
| Infrastructure (ports, roads, water) | Aboitiz InfraCapital (airports, economic estates, Apo Agua) |
| Data science & AI | Aboitiz Data Innovation / EquiMax |
| Financial backing | UnionBank + AEV balance sheet + BlackRock/GIP |
| Community operations | ACI (91% local hiring, top taxpayer in Claver) |

The platform is built. What is missing is the specific deal. GMC is that deal.

### Capital Deployment Context

- **₱105B capex budget in 2025** — 37% increase over 2024
- ₱78B allocated to power (RE expansion, LNG, BESS)
- ₱16B to infrastructure
- AEV core net income: ₱25.5B (FY 2025)
- AboitizPower EBITDA: ₱79.6B (+9% YoY)
- Revenue (TTM Sep 2025): ₱309.1B

They are not sitting on capital. They are actively looking for the right places to deploy it.

### The Processing-Not-Extraction Framing

The argument is NOT for Aboitiz to enter mining extraction (high political, environmental, and reputational risk). The argument is for **mineral processing and downstream beneficiation** — the middle of the value chain.

This aligns with:
- The techglomerate thesis (technology-driven, data-enabled)
- Existing capabilities (construction, maintenance, energy, infrastructure)
- Recurring revenue model (annuity-style earnings, exactly what the GT pursues)
- Manageable risk (processing with proven technology, not greenfield exploration)
- Nation-building language (Sabin's signature phrase)

### National Policy Tailwind

- **US-Philippines Critical Minerals MOU:** Signed February 2026 — cooperation on critical minerals supply chains
