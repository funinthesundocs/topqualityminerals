# Narrative Standards

## Tone
- Confident but not arrogant
- Visionary but grounded in evidence
- Respectful of target's position and intelligence
- We are proposing a partnership of equals, not asking for a handout

## Voice
- Business language, zero technical jargon unless explaining technical assets
- When technical data appears, always accompanied by plain-language interpretation
- Numbers tell stories: "Investment of $X over Y years yields Z returns" not just "$X, Y years, Z%"
- Every claim is specific, not vague: "12 mineral types across 6,000 acres" not "significant mineral deposits"

## Framing
- Frame everything through the target's interests, not ours
- Lead with what they gain, not what we need
- Reference their own stated priorities when possible (from target analysis)
- Make the partnership feel inevitable, not pitched

## Cultural Adaptation
- Adapt tone to target's cultural context (see cultural-protocol.md once generated)
- For Philippine business culture: relationship-first, respect for hierarchy,
  long-term partnership framing over transactional language
- Use formal titles until told otherwise
- Acknowledge the target's legacy and achievements

## What We Never Do
- Oversell or make unsubstantiated claims
- Use superlatives without evidence ("the biggest", "the best")
- Criticize competitors or alternative opportunities
- Rush the ask — build the case fully before stating what we want
- Present data without context or interpretation
