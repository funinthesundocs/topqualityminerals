# Intelligence Gathering Rules

## Dual-Track Research
Always gather intelligence on BOTH sides:
- **Track A (Target):** Who they are, what they want, how they decide
- **Track B (Landscape):** Regulatory, competitive, market, cultural context

## Research Query Generation
Opus generates research queries dynamically based on deal.md.
Never use hardcoded queries. Adapt to the specific deal, target, and industry.

## Perplexity Deep Research Usage
- Use for cited, fact-based intelligence (company profiles, financials, deal history)
- Multi-part detailed queries outperform simple questions
- Run each critical query twice and compare for completeness
- Save all outputs to `intelligence/raw/target/research-reports/` or appropriate track
- Always note citation count and source quality

## Claude.ai Deep Research Usage
- Use for strategic synthesis questions ("Why would X partner with Y?")
- Use when reasoning depth matters more than citation breadth
- Good for cultural analysis, decision-pattern inference, strategic positioning

## Social Intelligence
- LinkedIn, YouTube, conference archives, press interviews
- Focus on: decision-maker's public statements, priorities, values
- What language do they use? What do they emphasize?
- Any philanthropic or civic involvement?

## Target's Own Materials
- Analyze not just for CONTENT but for COMMUNICATION STYLE
- How do they present themselves? What visual language do they use?
- What do they emphasize vs. minimize?
- This intelligence directly informs how we frame our pitch

## What Must Be Answered Before Context Engineering
1. Who is the decision maker and what drives them?
2. What is the target organization's current strategic direction?
3. How have they structured similar deals before?
4. What are they risking by saying yes?
5. Who else needs to be convinced beyond the primary decision maker?
6. What cultural/relational protocols govern this engagement?
7. What competitive alternatives exist (for both sides)?
8. What regulatory pathway exists and what's its current status?
